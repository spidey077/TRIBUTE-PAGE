J. Robert Oppenheimer

THE MAN WHO INVENTED THE MOST DESTRUCTIVE THING

TIMELINE

  • April 22, 1904: Julius Robert Oppenheimer is born in New York City, USA.
  • 1921: Graduates from the Ethical Culture School in New York.
  • 1925: Completes his undergraduate studies in chemistry at Harvard University.
  • 1926: Begins studies at the University of Cambridge under J.J. Thomson but shifts focus to theoretical physics.
  • 1927: Transfers to the University of GÃ¶ttingen in Germany, where he earns his Ph.D. in physics under Max Born.
  • 1929: Returns to the United States and begins teaching at the University of California, Berkeley, and the California Institute of Technology.
  • 1930s: Becomes a prominent figure in quantum mechanics and theoretical physics.
  • 1942: Appointed as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, the U.S. government's secret project to develop the atomic bomb.
  • 1943: Moves to Los Alamos, New Mexico, to lead the laboratory where the bomb's development takes place.
  • July 16, 1945: Oversees the first successful test of an atomic bomb, codenamed "Trinity," in the New Mexico desert.
  • August 6 & 9, 1945: The atomic bombs developed under Oppenheimer's leadership are dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, Japan, contributing to the end of World War II.
  • 1945: Oppenheimer becomes a chief advisor to the newly-formed United States Atomic Energy Commission (AEC), advocating for international control of nuclear weapons and opposing the development of the hydrogen bomb.
  • 1953: Accused of being a security risk due to past associations with Communists, leading to a controversial security hearing.
  • 1954: Stripped of his security clearance, effectively ending his role in government nuclear policy.
  • 1954: Returns to academic life and continues to contribute to science and philosophy.
  • 1963: Awarded the Enrico Fermi Award by President Lyndon B. Johnson for his contributions to theoretical physics.
  • February 18, 1967: Robert Oppenheimer dies of throat cancer in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 62.
